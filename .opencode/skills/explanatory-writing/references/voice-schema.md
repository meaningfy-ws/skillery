# The 8-axis voice schema

A voice is hard to diff or reuse as a prose blob. Decompose it into eight measurable axes. This is the
diffable structure a captured profile uses, and the contract a future style-capture method consumes.

Four axes are realised by the craft moves in [`craft-moves.md`](craft-moves.md). The other four are
**owned by the house voice** — link
[`company-voice.md`](../../executive-communication/references/company-voice.md), do not restate it.

| # | Axis | Owned by | This skill's contribution |
|---|------|----------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Tone** | `company-voice.md` (SMILE: friendly, honest, assured, curious) | inherited; the craft must not push it warmer or more evangelical |
| 2 | **Sentence rhythm** | craft move #4 (short declaratives) | mixed length, broken by short standalone lines |
| 3 | **Vocabulary / jargon density** | craft move #6 (coin-and-explain) + `company-voice.md` (plain words) | domain terms allowed, always landed with an example or gloss |
| 4 | **Hook style** | craft move #3 (self-answered question) | a scene, a question, or a long-horizon framing — not a definition |
| 5 | **Punctuation density** | `company-voice.md` (no em dash; sparing rhetorical questions) | inherited; rhythm comes from short sentences, not dashes |
| 6 | **Argument structure** | craft move #1 (one controlling metaphor) + `executive-communication` (pyramid, when deciding) | one conceit carries an explainer; structure defers to `executive-communication` when a decision is in play |
| 7 | **Citation pattern** | craft move #2 (concrete-beside-abstract) | every abstract claim sits beside a named example; hard statistics need a source |
| 8 | **Closings** | craft move #7 (confident grounded close) | land the point, no hedge, no overclaiming |

## How to use it

- **Reading a voice:** score a sample on the eight axes to make its fingerprint explicit and diffable.
- **Writing in a voice:** axes 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 8 are the craft levers; axes 1 and 5 are inherited
  guardrails you must not drift.
- **Hand-off to capture (deferred EPIC):** a captured profile fills all eight axes from a real corpus;
  this schema is the shared shape so the two EPICs line up.
