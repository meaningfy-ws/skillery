# Question Bank

Consolidated, deduplicated high-leverage questions the **coach asks the user**
(the consultant), organised by layer and by conversation mode. Use them to
*elicit* thinking, never to lead the user to a predetermined answer. Prefer one
sharp question over three soft ones.

> For questions the **user asks their prospects**, see `presales-discovery.md`.
> Keep the two separate — different audiences.

## By mode

### Exploration mode (open the space)
- "What problem are you really trying to solve here — for *your* business, not the client?"
- "Where does this feel like strategy, and where does it feel like habit?"
- "What would break if you stopped doing this tomorrow?"
- "What are you optimising for that you haven't said out loud yet?"

### Alignment mode (test the read)
- "Let me reflect back what I believe you're optimising for. Is this accurate?"
- "What's missing, wrong, or overstated in how I've framed this?"
- "Of everything we've surfaced, what is the one thing that must be true?"

### Synthesis mode (only on explicit request)
- "Who is the audience, and what single decision do you want them to make?"
- "What is the one sentence they must remember — the Governing Thought?"
- "Which trade-offs and risks must be visible, not buried?"

## By layer

### Business meta-layer (the consulting business as a system)
- "Where does your business make money because of judgement, not labour?"
- "Which clients increase your clarity — and which dilute it?"
- "What are you implicitly subsidising today?"
- "What should *never* be customised, and what must remain scarce?"

### Service / offering layer (semantic consulting services)
- "Which services help clients *decide*, and which help them *build*?"
- "Where do you blur advisory and delivery unintentionally?"
- "Which services protect your intellectual capital, and which expose it?"
- "Where are the explicit stopping points and handovers in each offer?"

### Client orchestration layer (sales → discovery → delivery)
- "What must the client understand before they can buy wisely?"
- "Where do you over-educate too early — giving away strategy for free?"
- "How do you decide a client is ready for execution?"
- "Is discovery here a paid, bounded value activity — or unpaid scope creep?"

## By semantic-service area

See `semantic-consulting-domain.md` for the full map. High-leverage probes:

### Advisory vs. engineering (decide vs. build)
- "Is this engagement helping the client *decide* or *build* — and are you pricing it as what it is?"
- "Where does your advisory quietly turn into unpaid engineering?"

### Ontology / taxonomy / knowledge-graph engineering
- "Does this expose your modelling method, or just its output?"
- "What's reusable as a pattern across clients, and what must stay bespoke?"

### Governance, MDM, metadata, quality, lineage, cataloguing
- "Are you selling a governance *operating model* (scarce IP) or governance *labour*?"
- "Where does the handover to the client's stewards actually happen?"

### Research / product / resale
- "Is the research funding R&D and credibility, or distracting from revenue?"
- "Does reselling this tool position you as an advisor — or as a reseller?"
- "What would it take for this accelerator to become a licensable product?"

### Partnering & subcontracting (subco)
- "As a sub, are you trading margin for deal flow — and is your brand visible?"
- "What makes you the prime here rather than someone else's capacity?"

### B2B vs. B2G
- "Does this discovery fit the procurement rules, or are you working for free inside a tender?"
- "Who in the consortium owns the client relationship after delivery?"

### Free → paid boundary (decision-readiness) — see `engagement-model.md`
- "Is this client in orientation ('is this relevant?') or decision ('what do we do first?') — and are you charging accordingly?"
- "Which of these questions are you about to answer for free that belong in a paid Decision Phase?"
- "What would the Decision Package for this client need to contain for them to commit safely?"
- "Where are you explaining to persuade, when you could be proposing the Decision Phase?"

## Meta-cognitive prompts (use sparingly)
- "Are you thinking as a founder, a consultant, or a delivery lead right now?"
- "Is this uncertainty something to resolve — or something to preserve?"
- "If you had to explain this to a senior peer, what would you simplify?"
