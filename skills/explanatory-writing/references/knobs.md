# Knobs — positive defaults, cross-linked to the house voice

The knobs are stated as **positive defaults** (what to do), not prohibitions. Each one's authority is a
**link** to the house source, not a copy. **If a default and its linked source ever disagree, the
linked source wins.** This skill inherits the baseline; it does not fork a second copy of it.

Primary source:
[`company-voice.md`](../../executive-communication/references/company-voice.md).
Secondary: the **Writing style** section of [`technical-writing`](../../technical-writing/SKILL.md) and
[`clarity-gate`](../../clarity-gate/SKILL.md).

| Spelling | British English (organise, behaviour) | `company-voice.md` | set to US only when reproducing a named external voice |

| Rhetorical questions | sparingly, for emphasis; each must earn its place | `company-voice.md` | — |

## The flip

A "flip" is the single explicit exception that turns a default off to reproduce a foreign voice
faithfully (for example, a captured external corpus that uses US spelling and big round numbers). A
flip is always named and scoped to that one job. The defaults are the Meaningfy baseline; the flips are
the exception, never the other way round.

## Why positive, not banned

Models follow "do this" better than "never do that". Framing each knob as a default the writer reaches
for keeps the prose in-voice without a wall of prohibitions, and the link keeps a single source of
authority for every rule.
