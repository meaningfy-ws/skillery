# Statement of Work (SoW) — template

The fillable template for the **SoW**: the contractual companion to the [proposal](proposal-template.md).
Precise and enumerated, no marketing prose. Its **out-of-scope list is the same list** as the
exclusions in the [`estimation`](../../estimation/SKILL.md) worksheet — keep them in lock-step (see
the [scope-boundary checklist](scope-boundary-checklist.md)).

---

```markdown
# Statement of Work: <client / engagement>

- **Parties:** <provider> and <client>
- **Reference / version / date:**
- **Engagement phase:** P1 Decision Phase (paid, fixed frame)

## 1. Objective

<One sentence: deliver a Decision Package that makes the first commitment safe and informed.>

## 2. Deliverables

| # | Deliverable | Definition of done |
|---|-------------|--------------------|
| D1 | Decision Package | Recommendation, in/out scope, pilot→scale roadmap, buy/build/defer, execution brief (per `decision-package`) |
| D2 | <e.g. first-cut conceptual model sketch, if in scope> | <done criteria> |

## 3. Scope — in

- <activity / output> — <boundary>
- <activity / output> — <boundary>

## 4. Scope — out (load-bearing — = the estimate's exclusions)

- **Implementation / build (P2 Execution)** — separately scoped and priced.
- <exclusion> — <when it might return, as a future engagement>
- <exclusion> — <…>

> Each line here MUST match an exclusion in the `estimation` worksheet. If it was excluded from the
> number, it is excluded from the commitment.

## 5. Price and payment

- **Fixed-frame price:** <amount> (covers Section 3 only).
- **Payment schedule:** <e.g. on signature / on delivery>.
- Work beyond Section 3 is a separate, separately-priced engagement.

## 6. Assumptions

<The assumptions the estimate rests on — client inputs, access, availability, decisions. Drawn from
the `estimation` worksheet. If an assumption fails, the stop/extend mechanics in Section 8 apply.>

## 7. Inputs required from the client

- <data access, SME time, decision authority, source documents — and by when.>

## 8. Stop / extend mechanics

If agreed outcomes cannot be reached for reasons outside the provider's control, the provider will
signal it explicitly and offer: a scope reduction, a short time-boxed extension, or a clean stop with
partial delivery. No silent overwork.

## 9. Handover

P2 Execution (build/implementation) is a separate engagement with its own scope and budget. This SoW
commits only to delivering the Decision Package.
```
